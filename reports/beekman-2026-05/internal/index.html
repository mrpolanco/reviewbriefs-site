10 Strategic Recommendations

Fix premium booking benefit and checkout communication end-to-end
Critical priority 30 days

This is not a policy problem — it is a communication and training problem. The credits exist, the hotel can honor them, and at least one GM-level resolution resulted in a full refund. The gap is that front desk staff cannot resolve without escalation, and the escalation path fails. A policy change giving front-line managers authority to resolve credit disputes under $200 — combined with pre-arrival email clarity and updated FAQ content — would eliminate the top negative review driver. All 5+ affected guests had otherwise excellent stays. These are not anti-hotel reviewers — they are high-LTV guests the hotel turned into detractors over a recoverable billing moment.

Build a structured review response program with dedicated ownership and 48-hour SLA
High priority 30–45 days to clear backlog

Visible unanswered high-risk items are one of the most important reputation gaps in this dataset. Because owner response visibility varies by platform, treat the count as an audit queue rather than a formal response-rate metric. A named, personalized response to priority items converts a negative signal into evidence of responsive management.

Rewrite the homepage hero to anchor on the Victorian atrium as the primary conversion signal
